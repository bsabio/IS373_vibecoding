NeoBrutalism

Explore Works Read & Learn

Neo-brutalism in web design is a bold, raw visual style that intentionally rejects polished, ultra-refined interfaces in favor of an unfiltered, almost “unfinished” aesthetic. It draws inspiration from traditional brutalist architecture—focusing on honesty, functionality, and visible structure—by using thick borders, harsh edges, high-contrast colors, oversized typography, and simple, flat elements. Instead of subtle gradients, soft shadows, or rounded corners, neo-brutalist design leans into blunt shapes, asymmetry, and layouts that feel more like wireframes or experimental posters. The result is a visually striking, anti-corporate look that emphasizes clarity, personality, and a refreshingly direct user experience.

Core Principles of Neo‑Brutalism

Material Honesty

Expose materials and finishes — concrete, raw metals, and paper — without decorative masking.

Monumentality & Scale

Favor bold, large-scale forms and generous negative space to emphasize presence and mass.

Structural Expression

Reveal structure and joinery as compositional elements rather than hiding them behind ornament.

Typographic Rigour

Use strict grids, large type, and high-contrast typography to communicate hierarchy directly.

Raw Texture & Contrast

Pair smooth planes with coarse textures; embrace visible tooling, seams, and edges.

Functional Clarity

Let function drive form: clear systems, plain interfaces, and unapologetic utility.
